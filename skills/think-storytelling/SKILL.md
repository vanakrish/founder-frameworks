Covers storytelling, brand building, positioning narrative, and content-as-marketing.

**Dietrich Mateschitz (Red Bull):**
- "The money flows as a function of the stories. Learning to tell a story is incredibly important because that's how the money works."
- Red Bull is "not just a drink. It's a philosophy."
- Don't buy ads, create content — own the media your audience consumes
- Sports teams as media multipliers: "In value terms, the total editorial media value plus the media assets are superior to pure advertising"
- Brand designed to be "self-ironic, non-conformist, smart, and rebellious"
- "The most dangerous thing for a branded product is low interest" — obscurity kills
- Spent 18 months rejecting 50 campaign ideas before finding the right one
- "It is a must to believe in one's product" — belief must be genuine
- The demographic you want finds YOU through culture, not targeting

**Steve Jobs:**
- Learned from Walt Disney: "The art of storytelling is very old and no amount of technology can turn a bad story into a good story"
- Products as storytelling — the product IS the narrative
- "Make something with a great deal of care and love. It'll be obvious if you actually care."
- Digital products fade, but great stories endure — Snow White still generating $250M+ 60 years later
- "The world is a very malleable place. If you know what you want, you go for it with maximum energy and drive and passion."

**Larry Ellison:**
- Narrative creates reality: "Network computing architecture got no traction. Internet computing architecture got traction." Same product, different name.
- "We can't explain what we do unless we compare it to someone else" — positioning is relative
- Simple messages always win over complex explanations
- "If Detroit ran like Silicon Valley, nobody would sell cars. They'd just sell parts." — The right metaphor changes everything
- Pick your enemies carefully — they help define your story

**Process:**
1. Ask: What's your storytelling challenge? (Building a brand from scratch? Repositioning? Creating content? Pitching investors? Launching a product?)
2. Ask: Who is your audience? What do they care about?
3. Apply founder perspectives:
   - **Mateschitz**: What's the PHILOSOPHY behind your product? Not features — beliefs.
   - **Jobs**: Does your product tell a story through its design and experience?
   - **Ellison**: Can you reframe your market by naming it differently? Who's your enemy?
4. Universal truth: Authentic belief beats manufactured messaging every time
5. Output: Narrative strategy with positioning statement, philosophy articulation, content approach, and specific founder quotes
