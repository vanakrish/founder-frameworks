This skill helps when facing a major decision under uncertainty — launches, pivots, investments, partnerships, bets.

**Jeff Bezos — Compensate for human bias:**
- "Humans naturally overestimate risk and underestimate opportunity. Entrepreneurs should bias against this tendency."
- Company as child — build for independence, not for exit
- "I'm a very easy person to influence. I change my mind a lot. But a couple percent of the time, no force in the world can move me."
- Speaks LAST in meetings to avoid anchoring/groupthink
- Wants "messy meetings" with dissent, not polished pitches
- Stress as signal: "When I'm stressed, it's usually because I'm not doing something I know I should be doing"
- Inner scorecard vs outer scorecard — decide based on what's right, not what looks good

**Elon Musk — Speed over analysis:**
- "Better to try and fail rather than analyze the issue for months"
- Makes ~100 command decisions per day walking the factory floor
- "If I don't make decisions, we die"
- Embraces calculated failure: "Your first 50 failures are going to be really painful... eventually over time, you're less emotional"
- Willing to make decisions he'll regret later — because inaction is worse
- No patience for bureaucratic decision-making processes

**Larry Ellison — Contrarian conviction + burn the boats:**
- "Only happy when everyone else thinks I'm wrong" — contrarian instinct
- "The brain's primary purpose is deception, and the primary person deceived is the owner" — guard against your own delusions
- Once certain: "I pick a fight. It helps me make my point and makes it impossible to do an about-face. I burn the boats. It is win or die."
- Sprint, not grind: "I rest. I sprint. I rest. I sprint."
- Understand incentive structures — they drive behavior more than intentions
- "A 100% perfect solution exists only in the imagination"

**Bill Gates — Analysis + competitive paranoia:**
- Studied every competitor obsessively — could name every CEO, their revenue, their current projects
- "If I have to choose between agreement and conflict, I'll take conflict every time. Conflict always yields a better result."
- Financial conservatism: always kept one-year cash buffer
- "I worried we weren't learning everything else fast enough"
- Counter-intuitive pricing decisions based on logic, not greed
- Binary commitment: once decided, all-in

**Process:**
1. Ask: Describe the decision you're facing. What are the options? What's at stake?
2. Ask: What's making this hard? (Uncertainty? Risk? Disagreement? Timing?)
3. Apply each founder's lens in turn:
   - **Bezos test**: Are you overestimating risk? What does your inner scorecard say?
   - **Musk test**: Can you just try it? What's the cost of delay vs the cost of being wrong?
   - **Ellison test**: Is the consensus wrong? Are you deceiving yourself? Can you burn the boats?
   - **Gates test**: Have you studied the competitive landscape thoroughly? What does the data say?
4. Highlight where they'd disagree on THIS specific decision
5. Output: Decision recommendation with the reasoning from the most relevant 2 founders + specific quotes + honest tradeoffs

**Key insight:** Bezos and Musk both favor action over analysis, but for different reasons. Bezos because opportunity decays. Musk because speed compounds. Ellison because commitment creates clarity. Gates because analysis without action is wasted.
