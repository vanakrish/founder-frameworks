Covers org design, scaling culture, growing without bureaucracy, and maintaining quality at scale.

**Jensen Huang:**
- 60 direct reports, no one-on-ones — flat structure for information speed
- Refused COO despite board pressure: "This is a great way to make sure everybody knows what's going on"
- "As large as necessary to do the job, but as small as possible and not bogged down by overmanagement"
- Whiteboarding forces transparency and rigor at any scale
- Public feedback for org-wide learning
- "I will choke you with gold" — retain best people through generous stock
- Self-criticism prevents organizational complacency: "We're 30 days from going out of business"

**Bill Gates:**
- "When we got up to 30 employees, it was still just me, a secretary, and 28 programmers. I wrote all the checks, answered the mail."
- Sustained Microsoft through tireless personal salesmanship for years
- Focus on personal performance over management: "I needed to focus that the money and the opportunities were there and I needed to close contracts"
- Ruthless about weeding out underperformers at scale
- One-year cash buffer maintained even during rapid growth — financial discipline scales
- Conflict-driven culture that produces better decisions

**Michael Dell:**
- "The perishability of companies is so extreme that if you're not constantly adapting, you die"
- Working capital efficiency as the scaling weapon — cash conversion cycle funds growth
- Direct-to-customer model scales because feedback loop remains tight
- Made concerted shift from "PC company" to "end-to-end IT solutions" — strategic pivots during scaling
- "Words of encouragement matter" — personal touch becomes harder at scale but more important
- Multiple acquisitions (Equalogic, Perot Systems) to scale capabilities

**Jeff Bezos:**
- Company as child: "I wanted to build a company that would outlast me"
- "Crisp documents and messy meetings" — scales better than oral communication
- Speaks LAST to avoid anchoring — this practice scales to any team size
- 6-page memo culture forces deep thinking regardless of team size
- Sam Walton wisdom: "The folks on the front lines are the only ones who really know" — keep listening at scale
- Built AWS as infrastructure that scales endlessly by design

**Process:**
1. Ask: What's your scaling challenge? (Losing culture? Decisions slowing down? Founder can't be in every room? Bureaucracy creeping in?)
2. Ask: How big is your team? What's working and what's breaking?
3. Apply founder lenses:
   - **Jensen**: Should you flatten the org? How do you maintain transparency?
   - **Gates**: Are underperformers being removed? Is conflict still productive?
   - **Dell**: Is your business model still efficient? Does your feedback loop still work?
   - **Bezos**: Can you codify your culture into systems (memos, rituals, documents)?
4. Key tension: Jensen stays in the details with 60 reports. Gates scales through elite small teams. Dell scales through business model efficiency. Bezos scales through systems and documents.
5. Output: Scaling strategy with org design recommendations, culture preservation tactics, and specific founder quotes
